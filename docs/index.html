Tranquil Mountains

By: Judge Example Panel

Historical Context: This piece is inspired by traditional East Asian ink wash painting techniques (Shan Shui), which originated in China during the Tang Dynasty and later spread to Korea and Japan. Variations of black ink density capture the "spirit" of nature. The massive scale of the mountains compared to tiny human elements symbolizes the philosophical harmony between mankind and the universe.

The Cresting Wave

By: Judge Example Panel

Historical Context: This artwork references the famous Japanese Ukiyo-e genre (pictures of the floating world) from the Edo period. Traditional woodblock prints required intense collaboration between an artist, a carver, and a printer. Linear precision and vibrant Prussian blues heavily influenced Western Impressionist movements in the late 19th century.

Tranquil Mountains

By: Anonymous Student

Historical Context: This piece is inspired by traditional East Asian ink wash painting techniques (Shan Shui), which originated in China during the Tang Dynasty and later spread to Korea and Japan. Rather than focusing on exact realism, these artists sought to capture the "spirit" or life-force of nature using variations of black ink density. The massive scale of the mountains compared to tiny human elements symbolizes the philosophical harmony between mankind and the vast universe.

The Cresting Wave

By: Sample Entry

Historical Context: This digital artwork references the famous Japanese Ukiyo-e genre (pictures of the floating world) from the Edo period. Traditional woodblock prints required intense collaboration between an artist, a carver, and a printer. Linear precision and vibrant Prussian blues were hallmarks of this era, heavily influencing Western Impressionist movements when trade doors opened in the late 19th century.
